# Graph Neural Network for the XENON Detector

Fascinated by dark matter? Well, aren't we all? And I am thrilled that I got the opportunity to directly contribute to its research!

Located in Italy, the XENON detector was established to study potential dark matter candidates. Broadly, it is just a cylinder full of liquid Xenon with vacuum tubes (to detect photons) aligned in a hexagonal arrangement at the top and the bottom faces.

{% include elements/figure.html image="/assets/img/ucsd.jpg" caption="The XENON Detector" %}
<!-- ![preview](/assets/img/ucsd.jpg) -->

In fact, this hexagonal arrangement piqued the community’s interest to experiment with graph neural networks. After all, what better than graphs could capture a hexagonal shape!

I designed a graph neural network (comprising convolutional graph layers) to reconstruct the position of the collision happening in the detector. And my neural network performed better than the original software used by XENON to reconstruct the position!
